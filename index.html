Fact 1 of 5

The Five Good Emperors

Nerva, Trajan, Hadrian, Antoninus Pius, and Marcus Aurelius ruled Rome at its peak (96–180 CE). Gibbon called this perhaps the happiest period in human history — though slaves and conquered peoples might disagree. (The Rest is History)

Fact 2 of 5

Hadrian Builds His Wall

In 122 CE, Emperor Hadrian ordered construction of a 73-mile wall across northern Britain — not to stop invasion but to control movement, tax trade, and signal the limits of Roman ambition. It still stands.

Fact 3 of 5

The Canon Was Not Fixed (Ehrman)

In the 2nd century, Christians used dozens of gospels — the Gospels of Thomas, Peter, Philip, Mary Magdalene. There was no agreed Bible. Different communities used radically different texts.

Fact 4 of 5

Gnosticism: The Road Not Taken (Ehrman)

Second-century Gnostic Christians believed the material world was created by an evil lesser god. Jesus came to reveal secret spiritual knowledge for liberation. Their texts (Nag Hammadi library) survived buried in Egypt.

Fact 5 of 5

Marcus Aurelius: Philosopher-Emperor

Marcus Aurelius wrote his private 'Meditations' in Greek — a Stoic journal never intended for publication. Tom Holland calls him the closest the ancient world came to Plato's philosopher-king, yet he spent most of his reign fighting Germanic tribes.
