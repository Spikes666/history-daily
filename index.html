Fact 1 of 5

Nero's Persecution

Emperor Nero blamed Christians for the Great Fire of Rome in 64 CE — launching the first state persecution of Christians. Peter and Paul were likely executed during this period. (The Rest is History)

Fact 2 of 5

Revelation's Real Target (Ehrman)

Revelation, written around 95 CE, used coded imagery ('Babylon,' 'the Beast') to attack Rome and Emperor Domitian. Ehrman explains it was political resistance literature, not a prediction of 21st century events.

Fact 3 of 5

Augustus and the Pax Romana

Augustus ended a century of Roman civil war and inaugurated the Pax Romana — 200 years of relative peace. The Roman road network he expanded would later carry Paul's missionary journeys across the empire.

Fact 4 of 5

The Jewish Diaspora

After the Temple's destruction in 70 CE, Jews scattered across the Roman Empire. This diaspora produced Rabbinic Judaism, centered on Torah study rather than sacrifice — a transformation that made Judaism portable and survivable.

Fact 5 of 5

Roman Britain Begins

Emperor Claudius personally oversaw the invasion of Britain in 43 CE. Tom Holland notes the Romans were genuinely baffled by the ocean, which they saw as the edge of the mortal world. (The Rest is History)
