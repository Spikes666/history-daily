Fact 1 of 5

The Antonine Plague

A devastating pandemic (likely smallpox) struck the empire 165–180 CE, killing an estimated 5–10 million. Tom Holland notes it may have boosted Christianity — the church cared for the sick when pagan neighbors fled.

Fact 2 of 5

Marcion's Radical Bible (Ehrman)

Around 144 CE, Marcion proposed the first Christian 'canon' — rejecting the entire Old Testament. His heresy ironically prompted orthodox Christians to think seriously about which texts were authoritative.

Fact 3 of 5

Trajan's Column

Trajan's Column, completed 113 CE, spirals 200 scenes of the Dacian Wars — the world's first continuous narrative frieze. It was propaganda, spectacle, and historical record simultaneously.

undefined
Fact 4 of 5

The Silk Road Expands

By the 2nd century CE, Roman merchants traded directly with Han Dynasty China via the Silk Road — Roman glass found in Chinese tombs, Chinese silk found in Roman Egypt. The two empires never made formal contact.

undefined
Fact 5 of 5

Han China's Golden Age

The Han Dynasty governed 60 million people — larger than Rome. Paper was invented, the civil service exam institutionalized, and Confucianism made state ideology. Its collapse around 220 CE triggered China's Three Kingdoms period.
