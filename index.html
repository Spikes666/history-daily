Fact 1 of 5

The Historical Jesus (Ehrman)

Bart Ehrman argues Jesus was a real apocalyptic Jewish preacher who believed the end of the world was imminent. Our earliest sources — Paul's letters — were written 20 years after Jesus's death by someone who never met him.

Fact 2 of 5

The Gospels Were Not Eyewitness Accounts (Ehrman)

The four Gospels were written 35–65 years after Jesus's death, in Greek, by educated authors — not by Galilean fishermen. The attributions (Matthew, Mark, etc.) were added later by church tradition.

Fact 3 of 5

Scribal Errors and Alterations (Ehrman)

Ehrman's 'Misquoting Jesus' documents how early Christian scribes introduced thousands of changes to manuscripts — some accidental, some theologically motivated. We have no original manuscripts of any New Testament text.

Fact 4 of 5

The Destruction of Jerusalem

In 70 CE, Roman general Titus destroyed the Second Temple — ending Temple Judaism and reshaping both Judaism and nascent Christianity. The Arch of Titus in Rome still depicts the looting of the Menorah. (The Rest is History)

undefined
Fact 5 of 5

Paul: Christianity's True Architect (Ehrman)

Ehrman argues Paul of Tarsus built the theological framework of Christianity — the idea of Jesus as cosmic divine figure whose death atoned for sin. Paul wrote before any Gospel and never heard Jesus preach.
